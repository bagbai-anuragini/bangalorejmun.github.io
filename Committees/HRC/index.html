UN Human Rights Council

Agenda: Role of Education in Combating Exploitation

The United Nations Human Rights Council is an intergovernmental body responsible for promoting and protecting human rights. The 47 seats are filled by elected members that serve for three years. It is a subsidiary body to the United Nations General Assembly. It works closely with the United Nations Office of High Commissioner for Human Rights. With the current rise in human trafficking, the exploitation of children is much greater than before. Due to forced child labour, many children around the world have been denied the right to a basic education. Education has the power to easily change the views, beliefs, and opinions of a large community.

This year at JMUN, the delegates of the Human Rights Council will be discussing the role of education in combating the exploitation of children with the aim of achieving Sustainable Development Goals 4 and 5.
