United Nations Security Council

Agenda: The Threat Posed By The Korean Crisis

The Security Council is arguably the most powerful organ of the UN. It has the responsibility of maintaining international peace and security, the very essence of the United Nations. Its powers include the establishment of peacekeeping operations, international sanctions and the authorization of military action through Security Council Resolutions. It is the only UN body that has the authority to issue binding resolutions to member states.

The UNSC will be discussing the threats posed by the ongoing Korean crisis with the aim of achieving the Sustainable Development Goal 16, which is to promote peaceful and inclusive societies for sustainable development, provide access to justice for all and build effective, accountable and inclusive institutions at all levels.
