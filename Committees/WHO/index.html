World Health Organization

Agenda: Implications of a Potential Water Crisis

“The future is green energy, sustainable, renewable energy.” - Arnold Schwarzenegger

“When the well's dry we know the worth of water.” - Benjamin Franklin


​The World Health Organization is a specialized agency of the UN that is concerned with international public health. It is headquartered in Geneva, Switzerland and is a member of the United Nations Development Group.

​The originating principle of nature and the nature of matter is a single material substance: water. Water is the most important facet of life on earth and it is running out. Nearly 70% of the Earth is covered in water but only 2.5% of it is freshwater and just 1% of the freshwater is easily accessible.


As delegates of the WHO you will be addressing the more important than ever Sustainable Development Goal 6 - to ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all - by discussing the imminent water crisis that could very soon be the most pressing problem of the 21st century if not dealt with immediately.
