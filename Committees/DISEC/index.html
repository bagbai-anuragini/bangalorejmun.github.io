Disarmament and International Security Council

Agenda: Evaluating the implications of militarizing international borders
The Disarmament and International Security Council Committee (DISEC), is the first committee of the United Nations General Assembly. It deals with disarmament, global challenges and threats to peace that affect the international community and seeks out solutions to the challenges in the international security regime.

DISEC and Bangalore JMUN 2018 will provide delegates with the rare opportunity to experience the complex interplay of the different UN member nations. The agenda for this year is to evaluate the implications of militarizing international borders. There is no doubt that border security is an important and increasingly pressing issue as countries invest more and more on protecting their borders. Consequently it becomes important that we ensure all countries maintain global peace and international security, preventing possible future catastrophes. DISEC demands delegates who are thorough with international relations of their country and other countries too, delegates who have a flair for debate and a penchant for parley.
